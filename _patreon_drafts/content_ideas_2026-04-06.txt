Content Ideas — 6 April 2026
Research from YouTube, TikTok, and web trends

---

1. "Bobby Rush / Sinners" Reaction + Lesson Tie-In

Bobby Rush (age 92) is everywhere right now — he played harmonica for the character Delta Slim in the film "Sinners," which got 16 Oscar nominations. A new documentary "King of the Chitlin' Circuit" is coming to Mississippi Public Broadcasting in August. This is a huge moment for blues harmonica in mainstream culture.

Content angle: Record a TikTok reacting to the Bobby Rush / Sinners clip. Talk about the Chitlin' Circuit, juke joint tradition, and how this connects directly to the Juke Joint Trail on HarpShed. Could be a 60-second "Did you know the harmonica in Sinners was played by a 92-year-old blues legend?" hook.

Why it works: Sinners is a cultural moment — tapping into it brings new eyes from film fans, not just harmonica players.

---

2. "1930s Delta Blues Harmonica" Lo-Fi Ambient Style

There's a wave of YouTube channels posting long-form "lost recordings" style content — vintage-sounding delta blues harmonica over crackling audio. Channels like "Whiskey Delta Blues" are getting views with titles like "Lost Blues Recordings 1930s – Delta Harmonica Session." The aesthetic is sepia/grain/porch footage.

Content angle: Record a short lo-fi "field recording" style video of yourself playing a Fox Chase or country blues piece. Lean into the HarpShed visual style — sepia, old-timey, porch feel. On TikTok, keep it 30-45 seconds with no talking, just playing. On YouTube, do a longer 5-10 minute ambient version. Caption: "If DeFord Bailey had a TikTok."

Why it works: Lo-fi/ambient blues is trending on YouTube. Your aesthetic already fits this perfectly. Low production effort, high shareability.

---

3. "One Riff, Three Levels" Beginner Hook

Beginner harmonica tutorials are consistently the most-searched harmonica content on TikTok. Isaac Corbitt and other creators are getting strong engagement with quick "here's how to play X" content. The trending format is showing a riff at beginner/intermediate/advanced levels.

Content angle: Pick a riff from one of your tunes (Old Joe Clark or Rain Crow Bill work well). Show it three ways — simple melody, then with bends, then with full tongue blocking and octave splits. 15-30 seconds per level. End card: "Want the full tab? Link in bio — HarpShed / Patreon."

Why it works: The "levels" format is proven on TikTok across instruments. It showcases your skill gap (aspirational) while making beginners feel welcome. Directly funnels to Patreon for the full breakdown.
