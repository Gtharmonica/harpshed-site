New Tune Drop: Yew Piney Mountain

There's a handful of fiddle tunes that just sound like the hills they came from. Yew Piney Mountain is one of them — a West Virginia tune believed to have been composed by Uncle Jack McElwain of Webster County, and it carries that modal, slightly crooked feel that makes Appalachian music so distinct.

This version comes from Grant Dermody's 2003 album Crossing That River. It's played in the key of A on a Low D harmonica, 2nd position. The A part sits in the upper middle register with a rolling, almost unsettled quality. The B part drops down into the low register with octave splits and a driving rhythmic pulse — a completely different colour that grounds the whole tune.
